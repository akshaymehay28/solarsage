# Electroluminescence (EL) Imaging

EL imaging drives current through a module in the dark and photographs the
near-infrared light silicon emits in forward bias. Regions that do not emit
are electrically dead or disconnected, making EL the definitive tool for
seeing cell cracks, broken interconnects, and inactive areas invisible to
the eye and often invisible to IR thermography.

## What EL reveals

Microcracks appear as dark lines; their orientation hints at cause
(dendritic patterns radiating from a point suggest impact, parallel cracks
across many modules suggest transport or handling stress). Dark isolated
regions show where cracks have severed the cell's fingers and disconnected
area - these correlate directly with power loss. Fully dark cells indicate
severe shunting or interconnect failure. PID shows a characteristic pattern
of darker cells nearest the frame edge. Broken busbars or ribbon
interconnects appear as darkened stripes.

## Field vs lab

Lab EL is done on flash tables during factory audits and claims disputes.
Field EL is increasingly practical: crews image strings at night using the
inverter or a portable supply to bias modules, or use daylight EL with
lock-in cameras. Field campaigns typically sample - imaging every module in
a large plant is rarely economic - guided by monitoring data and IR results.

## When to use EL

After hail events or suspected transport damage (before accepting delivery
where possible), to confirm PID diagnosis, to assess crack progression after
storms, and to settle warranty claims where power loss must be attributed to
manufacturing versus site handling.
