# Potential Induced Degradation (PID)

Potential induced degradation (PID) is a performance-loss mechanism in
photovoltaic modules caused by high system voltages driving ion migration —
primarily sodium ions from the cover glass — into the cell structure. It is
most severe at the negative end of long strings in ungrounded or
transformerless inverter systems, and is accelerated by heat and humidity.

## Symptoms

PID typically appears as a gradual, string-level power loss that worsens over
months. Affected modules show reduced shunt resistance, lower fill factor,
and depressed open-circuit voltage. Power output losses of 10-30% are common
in affected strings, and severe cases can exceed 50%. Electroluminescence
imaging shows a characteristic checkerboard pattern of darkened cells,
strongest nearest the frame.

## Detection and recovery

PID can be confirmed by comparing IV curves of suspect modules against
baseline, or by EL imaging. Because the underlying ion migration is partially
reversible, applying reverse voltage overnight (anti-PID boxes) can recover
much of the lost output. Long-term mitigation includes PID-resistant modules,
grounding the negative pole where the inverter topology allows, and reducing
string voltage.
