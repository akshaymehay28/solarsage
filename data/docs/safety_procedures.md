# Electrical Safety in PV Operations

PV arrays cannot be switched off: modules energise whenever light falls on
them, so DC circuits must be treated as live during all daylight work.

## Core hazards

String voltages in modern plants reach 1000-1500 V DC. DC arcs do not
self-extinguish, making load-break errors severe: never separate a PV
connector under load - open the DC disconnect or isolate at the inverter
first, and verify zero current with a clamp meter before unmating anything.
Capacitors in inverters hold charge after isolation; observe the
manufacturer's discharge wait time before opening enclosures.

## Lockout and verification

Apply lock-out/tag-out at every point of isolation, test-before-touch with a
meter rated for the DC voltage class (CAT III/1500 V), and prove the meter on
a known source before and after. Remember both sides of an isolator can be
live in a PV system - the array side stays energised in daylight regardless
of switch position.

## Non-electrical hazards

Roof work and fall protection dominate injury statistics in commercial PV.
Heat stress during summer maintenance, manual handling of modules in wind,
and wildlife (stinging insects nesting in trackers and combiner boxes) all
belong in the job hazard analysis.

## Emergency knowledge

Crews must know the site's rapid-shutdown provisions, the location of every
DC and AC isolation point, and that water application to an energised array
is a last resort governed by fire-service protocols, not O&M staff.
