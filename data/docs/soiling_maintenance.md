# Soiling and Cleaning Maintenance

Soiling is the accumulation of dust, pollen, bird droppings, and other debris
on module surfaces. It reduces the irradiance reaching the cells and is one
of the most common causes of underperformance in operational PV plants.

## Impact on output

Uniform dust accumulation typically reduces output by 2-7% in temperate
climates, but losses can exceed 20% in arid, high-dust regions or near
agricultural and industrial sites. Non-uniform soiling (such as bird
droppings or leaf debris) is more damaging than uniform dust because it can
create partial shading and localised heating on individual cells.

## Cleaning schedule

In most temperate climates, natural rainfall keeps soiling losses low and
one or two cleans per year are sufficient. Sites should be cleaned when the
measured soiling loss exceeds the cost of cleaning — typically at 3-5%
sustained loss. Panels should be cleaned in the early morning or evening with
deionised water and soft brushes; never use abrasive materials or high-pressure
jets, which can damage the anti-reflective coating and seals.
