# Preventive Maintenance Scheduling

A preventive maintenance (PM) programme balances scheduled work against
condition-based response driven by monitoring. Over-maintaining wastes
money; under-maintaining converts cheap fixes into expensive failures.

## A representative annual cycle (temperate utility-scale)

Continuous: automated monitoring with daily review of alerts, weekly review
of string-level trends and performance ratio.

Quarterly: visual inspection sample (modules, cables, connectors, fencing,
erosion), vegetation assessment, inverter filter check in dusty seasons,
irradiance sensor cleaning.

Semi-annual: torque-check sample of electrical terminations, thermographic
scan of switchgear and inverter connections, tracker lubrication where
fitted.

Annual: full drone IR survey of the array under high irradiance, IV curve
traces of a string sample plus all strings flagged by monitoring, inverter
service per manufacturer schedule, relay and protection testing, grounding
system spot checks, module cleaning if soiling analysis justifies it.

Event-driven: post-storm inspections (hail, wind above design thresholds,
lightning), EL sampling after hail, re-commissioning checks after any
re-wiring.

## Prioritising with monitoring data

Rank corrective work by estimated energy loss times expected time-to-repair.
A failed central inverter outranks everything; a single underperforming
string can often wait for the next scheduled visit. Track mean time to
repair by fault class - it is the O&M metric owners notice most after
availability itself.
