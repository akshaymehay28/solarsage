# Shading and Vegetation Management

## Why partial shading is disproportionately harmful

PV cells in a string carry the same current, so shading a single cell limits
the current of the entire substring until its bypass diode conducts - a small
shadow can remove a third of a module's output. Persistent partial shading
also drives hot-spot heating in the shaded cell as it dissipates the string's
current.

## Sources and signatures

Fixed obstructions (new buildings, poles, antennas) produce a shade pattern
that repeats at the same solar time daily and shifts seasonally - visible in
data as a reproducible dip in specific strings. Vegetation growth produces a
slowly worsening morning or evening loss over weeks. Row-to-row self-shading
in winter, when the sun is low, is a design property, not a fault; it appears
as symmetrical early/late losses in the cold months.

Inter-row shading loss and vegetation loss both concentrate at low sun
angles, but vegetation typically affects the lower module edge and the
strings nearest the perimeter.

## Management

Schedule vegetation control by growth season rather than a fixed calendar -
two cuts a year in temperate climates, more where growth is rapid. Graze
sheep where compatible. For fixed obstructions, options are module-level
power electronics (optimisers) on the affected strings, re-stringing so
shaded modules share a string, or accepting the modelled loss. Verify any
new shading against the site's original shade study before treating it as
degradation.
